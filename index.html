About Me.

When I first started college, I intended to become a dentist. I went through all the rigorous course work, achieving an associate's degree in Pre-Dental Sciences, and working hard to ace all my pre-reqs for dental school. By the time I finished all the required coursework, I was in the middle of getting a bachelor's in Cinema Studies before applying to dental school. At this point I realized that, while I had proven I could work hard and eventually go into dentistry, It wasn't something I was passionate about. I did quite well in my pre-dental classes such as majors physics and organic chemistry, but I didn't get excited about them. Somewhere along the way, I took a minor computer science course to fill a math credit toward my degree, and I found it fascinating, so I started to teach myself Python. 2 years later, I had finished my degree and was ready to apply for dental school, but by that point, my course was set. I knew I wanted to go into software development.
